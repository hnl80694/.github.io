ATLANTA BRAVES

History

The Atlanta Braves first started in 1871 as the Boston Red Stockings. In 1912, this team had be transformed into the Boston Braves. The Miracle Braves of 1914 were one of the most well-known teams in baseball history. In 1952, the Braves lost a lot of their fans to the Boston Red Sox. Milkwaukee was a city that wanted to attract a major league team and in 1953, the Braves moved to Milwaukee. Finally, in 1966, the Braves moved to Atlanta and started their winning ways again. They struggled in the 1980s, but in the 1990s, the Braves were delivering. The Braves were one of the most successdul Major League Baseball teams of the decade.

Timeline

Year Description
1876 The Red Stockings joined the National League and played their first game on April 22. They beat the Philadelphia Athletics, 6-5.
1883 The Braves are known as the Beaneaters to avoid their confusion with the Cincinnati Reds. The team wins the NL again.
1891 The Beaneaters go 87-51 and win the NL pennant. Chicago protested their win, but on November 11, the league ruled the pennant belonged to Beaneaters.
1903-1912 This is the era were the Beaneaters are not as successful as in the past and finish no better than 6th in the past 10 seasons. In 1907, they change their name to Doves, and in 1912, they get the nickname Braves.
1914 The Braves started off with a rocky start, but rallied through and ended up winning the NL pennant and the World Series.
1935 Babe Ruth finsihed his career in a Braves uniform. He removed himself from the lineup in a June game and officially retired. The Braves were 38-115 that season.
1953 The Braves play their first season in Milwaukee. They finished 2nd.
1954 Bobby Thomson suffered an ankle injury and the Braves were forced to try out outfielder Hank Aaron. The Braves finished third this season.
1966 The Braves start their season in their new home of Atlanta. The Braves finsihed fifth, but Aaron hit 44 homers to lead the league.
1982 Hank Aaron was elected to the Hall of Fame.
1993 The Braves become the first team to win three NL pennants back to back.
1995 The Braves win their first World Series in 38 years. The Braves were the first franchise to win the World Series in three different cities. The Braves had star players this season including; Greg Maddux, Mark Wohlers, Fred McGriff, and Chipper Jones.
1996 The Braves made it to the World Series again, but fell to the Yankees. This was their final season in the Atlanta-Fulton Stadium. John Smoltz thrived with a 2.94 ERA. Greg Maddux ended with a 2.72 ERA, and Tom Glavine with a 2.98 ERA. Chipper Jones and Fred McGriff were key hitters at the plate this season. Andruw Jones made his debut at just 19 years old.
1997 The Braves move to Turner Field.
2002 Manager, Bobby Cox, became the first National League manager to record at least 100 or more wins five different times. HE guided the team to its 11th consecutive division title.
2009 The Braves said goodbye to both Smoltz and Glavine. They traded Jeff Francoeur, which created an upset in Atlanta.
2010 This was Bobby Cox's final season as Braves manager. The Braves pulled through to win the wild card. Jason Heyward was a big hit in Atlanta this season and made it to the all stars.
2012 This season was when Chipper Jones retired and him htting a walk-off homerun in September became one of the most memorable events of the season.
2016 Rebuilding processes were still occur during this season. The Braves ended last in the National League East for the second straight time. Fredi Gonzalez became the first Atlanta manager to be fired since 1990. Although the season was not the best, Freddie Freeman and Julio Teheran earned good stats for themselves. This was the last season in Turner Field,
2017 The Braves start their 2017 season at a new ball field named Suntrust Park.
